\subparagraph{Context.} 
This problem investigates the influence of the nonlinear Wagner term \eqref{eq:wagner-gl-strain-pi} on lateral buckling. 

A closed form solution for the critical linearized buckling moment is \citep[(7.22)]{trahair1993flexuraltorsional}:
\[
\begin{aligned}
M_{\mathrm{cr}} & =\frac{\pi}{L}\sqrt{E I_y \TwistRigidity} \left[\left(1+\frac{\pi^2 \beta_x^2 E I_y}{4L^2 G \Jo}\right)^{1 / 2}+\frac{\pi \beta_x}{2 L}\left(\frac{E I_y}{\TwistRigidity}\right)^{1 / 2}\right] .
\end{aligned}
\]
where
\[
\beta_x \coloneq \frac{1}{I_x}\int y r^2\dA - 2y_0
\]

\subparagraph{Setup.}
A simply supported span of length $L = 6\unit{\meter}$ is subjected to constant flexure (\cref{fig:1035-model}). 

The span is discretized by 10 elements.
