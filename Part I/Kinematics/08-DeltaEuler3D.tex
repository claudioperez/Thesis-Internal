
The Euler model is obtained by enforcing $\gamma_2 = \gamma_3 = 0$. 
Using the expressions for $\gamma_2$ and $\gamma_3$ obtained for the second-order shear theory furnishes the system of equations:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\gamma_2 &= \theta_2^{\prime}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\theta_3^{\prime} \theta_1-\theta_3 \theta_1^{\prime}\right) =0 \\
\gamma_3 &= \theta_3^{\prime}+\frac{1}{2}\left(\theta_1^{\prime} \theta_2-\theta_1 \theta_2^{\prime}\right) =0 \\
\end{aligned}
$$
Using the expressions for $\gamma_2$ and $\gamma_3$ obtained for the 
second-order shear theory furnishes a linear system of equations,
whose solution is nonlinear in the kinematic variables. 
%
Linearizing this solution furnishes:
\begin{equation}
\bm{\theta} \approx \vartheta \FrameBasis  + (1-u_x^{\prime})\FrameBasis \times\bar{\bm{u}}^{\prime} + \frac{1}{2} (\FrameBasis \times\bar{\bm{u}}^{\prime}) \times \vartheta \FrameBasis 
\label{eq:delta-euler-theta}
\end{equation}
with components on the $\FrameBasis_{\beta}$ basis:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta_y \approx -u_{z}^{\prime}+\frac{1}{2} u_{y}^{\prime} \vartheta + u_{x}^{\prime} u_{z}^{\prime},
\quad 
\theta_{\FrameZi} \approx  u_{y}^{\prime}+\frac{1}{2} u_{z}^{\prime} \vartheta - u_{x}^{\prime} u_{y}^{\prime}
\end{aligned}
$$

\noindent Consider the identity:
$$
(a_0 \bm{1} + a_2 \, \mathbf{e}^{\times})^{-1}=\beta_0 \mathbf{1}+\beta_1 \mathbf{e} \otimes \mathbf{e}+\beta_2 \tilde{\mathbf{\Psi}} 
$$
with
$$
\begin{aligned}
\beta_0 &=\frac{a_0}{a_2^2+a_0^2} \\
\beta_1 &=\frac{a_2^2}{\left(a_2^2+a_0^2\right)a_0} \\
\beta_2 &=\frac{-a_2}{a_2^2+a_0^2} 
\end{aligned}
$$
Taylor expanding around $a_0 = 0$ and $a_2 = 1$ furnishes:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\beta_0(\varepsilon a_0, \varepsilon a_2) &=\frac{a_0}{a_2^2+a_0^2} 
= a_0 - 2 a_0 (a_2-1) + O(\varepsilon^3)\\
\beta_2 &=\frac{-a_2}{a_2^2+a_0^2} 
= a_2 - 2 + O(\varepsilon^3)
\end{aligned}
$$
