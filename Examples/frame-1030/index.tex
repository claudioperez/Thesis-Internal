\begin{Note} 
This example addresses a cantilever beam with asymmetric cross section subjected to an eccentric axial force $\AxialRxn$. 
The cantilever is fixed at the left end in Fig. 19. 
The axial force $P$ is applied to the shear center of the free end. 
As shown in Fig. 19, the geometric and material properties of the cantilever are: 
$L = 1,400 mm$, $a = 76 mm$, $b = 51 mm$, $t = 6.5$ mm. 

This example was simulated by several researchers [23,30,51]
The load–deflection curves of the present study together with the results of \cite{alsafadie2010corotational} are plotted in \cref{fig:1030-soln}, in which the axial displacement $u$ and lateral displacement $v$ in the $Y$ direction are presented. 
As seen in this figure, three new displacement-based elements in the present study are enough to obtain relatively accurate results. 
\cite{alsafadie2010corotational} utilized 30 displacement-based elements to get similar results.
\end{Note}
