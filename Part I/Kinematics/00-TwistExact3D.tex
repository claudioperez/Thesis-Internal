The present analysis will be based on the geometrically-exact equilibrium equations of classical (shear-free) beam theory as augmented with the Vlasov effect. One can write these equations in a compact form as demonstrated in Section 3.7 and easily augment them with the Vlasov effect to yield

$$
\left.
\begin{aligned}
N^{\prime}\,-\kappa_1 V_1-\kappa_2 V_2+p_t=0 \\
V_1^{\prime}+\kappa_1 N\,- \tau V_2 + p_1 = 0 \\
V_2^{\prime}+\kappa_2 V_t+ \tau V_1 + \bm{f}_n\cdot \mathbf{d}_2 = 0 \\
T^{\prime}\,+\kappa_1 M_2-\kappa_2 M_1+\gamma_1 V_2-\gamma_2 V_1 + m_t=0 \\
M_1^{\prime}+\kappa_2 T\, - \tau M_2 + \left(1+\epsilon\right) V_1-\gamma_1 N\,+m_1=0 \\
M_2^{\prime}-\kappa_1 T\, + \tau M_1 + \left(1+\epsilon\right) V_2-\gamma_2 N\,+m_2=0
\end{aligned}
\right\}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{r}
N^{\prime}-F_2 \kappa_3+F_3 \kappa_2=0 \\
F_2^{\prime}-F_3 \kappa_1+N \kappa_3=0 \\
F_3^{\prime}-N \kappa_2+F_2 \kappa_1=0 \\
M_1^{\prime}-M_2 \kappa_3+M_3 \kappa_2-Q_1^{\prime \prime}=0 \\
M_2^{\prime}-M_3 \kappa_1+M_1 \kappa_3-(1+\varepsilon) F_3-\kappa_3 Q_1^{\prime}=0 \\
M_3^{\prime}-M_1 \kappa_2+M_2 \kappa_1+(1+\varepsilon) F_2+\kappa_2 Q_1^{\prime}=0
\end{array}
