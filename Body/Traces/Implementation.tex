\section{Enhanced Shapes}\label{sec:trace-shapes}
In view of the developments in \cref{sec:trace}, any Class~I (including Class~II and Class~III as subsets) trace is representable without any auxiliary fields by a finite element that models the system of Box~\ref{box:basic-shear}. 
However, the consistency condition \eqref{eq:enhan-cii} is only meaningful for Class~II (with Class~III as a subset). 
Thus, in the following developments we derive enhanced strain shapes of the form \eqref{eq:enhan-strain} 
for the general Class~I family parameterized by \cref{tab:trace-consts} and only impose \eqref{eq:enhan-cii} for traces of Class~II. 
The resulting section model is generally non-variational for any trace outside of Class~II. 
% Thus the complete iterative procedure of Box~\ref{box:impl} which imposes \eqref{eq:enhan-cii}.


In the absence of auxiliary warping fields, the  linearized compatible section kinematic operator \eqref{eq:def-section-kinematic-matrix-Phi} is
\begin{equation*}
\begin{aligned}
\RigidStrainMatrix(\bm{r}) &:= \begin{bmatrix} 
\mathbf{1} & -\bm{r}^{\times}
\end{bmatrix}_{3\times6} 
\end{aligned}
\end{equation*}
In view of the Saint~Venant strain field \eqref{eq:iesan-point-strain}, the enhanced shapes must take the form:
\begin{SaveEquation}{eq:uniform-shapes}
\EnhanShapes = \RigidStrainMatrix\RigidShift + \WarpStrainMatrix\ShapeShift
\qquad\text{with}\qquad
\WarpStrainMatrix(\bm{r}) := 
\begin{bmatrix}
\nabla\WarpTwistIesan
&\FrameBasis_{\beta}\otimes\nabla_{\beta}\WarpShearShape{} + \PlaneShearShape
\end{bmatrix}_{3\times 3}
\end{SaveEquation}
where \(\RigidShift\) and \(\ShapeShift\) are constant matrices, and the functions \(\WarpTwistIesan\), \(\WarpShearShape{}\) and \(\PlaneShearShape\) are defined by \eqref{eq:bvp-warp-twist-iesan}, \eqref{eq:bvp-warp-shear-iesan}, and  \eqref{eq:def-plane-shear-shape}, respectively.
% Because this contains the plane-rigid span \(\PlaneMotions\), the primary trace fields \(\bm{u},\bm{\theta}\) 
% will not generally represent the full plane-rigid content, which becomes offloaded onto the warping field \(\bm\alpha\) through a linear operator \(\RigidShift\) determined by the trace. 
% Upon elimination of the warping fields \(\bm\alpha\) the material strains are 
% \begin{SaveEquation}{eq:eps-B-e}
% \StrainVector(\reflenx,\bm{r}) = \Big(\RigidStrainMatrix  + \RigidStrainMatrix\RigidShift + \WarpStrainMatrix \ShapeShift\Big) \,\bm{e}(\reflenx)
% \end{SaveEquation}
Because all Class~I traces generate deformations \(\TraceStrain\) that evolve with the form \eqref{eq:def-T0}, 
it is sufficient to match the \(\reflenx\)-constant strain field represented by \(\bm{\varepsilon}(0,\bm{r})\):
\begin{equation}
\bm{\varepsilon}(0,\bm{r}) = \Big(\RigidStrainMatrix (\bm{r})(\mathbf{1} + \RigidShift) + \WarpStrainMatrix(\bm{r})\ShapeShift\Big) \,\TraceDeDpRoot \bm{p}
\label{eq:trace-strain}
\end{equation}
Equating the \(\reflenx\)-constant strains in \eqref{eq:iesan-point-strain} with \eqref{eq:trace-strain} and exploiting the block structure of \(\TraceDpDeRoot\) \eqref{eq:T0-inv} furnishes after a lengthy but otherwise straightforward manipulation:
\begin{equation}
\RigidShift = \begin{bmatrix}
\FrameBasis\otimes\ShiftN - \oneS & \ShiftScaleN\FrameBasis\otimes\FrameBasis %+ \FrameBasis\otimes(\CenterTrace\times\FrameBasis) 
\\
-\FrameBasis^{\times}\ShiftA + %\FrameBasis\otimes\ShiftNK + 
\FrameBasis\otimes\ShiftB^{\top}(\CenterShearLocation +\ShiftT)
& (\ShiftScaleT + \ShiftK\cdot\CenterShearLocation - 1)\FrameBasis\otimes\FrameBasis + (\ShiftF\times\FrameBasis)\otimes\FrameBasis
\end{bmatrix}_{6\times6}
\label{eq:class-ii-Le}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
\ShapeShift = 
\begin{bmatrix}
(\ShiftB^{\top}\CenterShearLocation + \ShiftT)^{\top} & (\CenterShearLocation\cdot\ShiftK + \ShiftScaleT)\FrameBasis^{\top} \\
\ShiftB & \ShiftK\otimes\FrameBasis
\end{bmatrix}_{3\times6}
\label{eq:class-i-La}
\end{equation}
