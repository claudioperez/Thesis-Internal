This work presents contributions to the field of computational mechanics that target advances in structural health monitoring. 

The work is presented in two parts. 

Part~I collects several developments for efficient simulation of structures comprising slender structural members. 
The classical mathematical technique of dimensional reduction \citep{kantorovich1958approximate} is used to reduce the governing partial differential equations of continuum mechanics to a one-dimensional \emph{beam theory}, which is amendable to techniques of methods of finite element analysis.
