\subsection{Formulation}
Consider the linear equilibrium equations (Box~\ref{box:basic-shear}):
\begin{equation}
    \begin{aligned}
        \bm{n}' &=  \bm{0} \\
        \bm{m}' + \FrameBasis \times \bm{n} &= \bm{0} \\
        \bm{w}' - \bm{v} &= \bm{0}
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Let
\begin{equation*}
\bm{m}(x) = \phi_I(x) \bm{m}_I + \phi_J(x) \bm{m}_J + \bm{m}_{\mathrm{p}}(x)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\bm{w}(x) = \phi_I \bm{w}_I + \phi_J \bm{w}_J
\end{equation*}
%
The solution is expressed as:

$$
\bm{s}_h(x) = \mathbf{b}(x) \bm{q} + \bm{s}_{\mathrm{p}}(x)
$$
where the matrix $\mathbf{b}(x)$ contains resultant interpolation functions that express section forces in terms of basic forces. 
\begin{equation}
\begin{pmatrix}
N(x)   \\
V_y(x) \\
V_z(x) \\
T(x)   \\
M_z(x) \\
M_y(x) \\
B(x) \\
Q(x) \\
\end{pmatrix}
=\left[\begin{array}{ccc|ccc|cc}
1 &             0 &            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & & \\
0 &  \frac{1}{L} & \frac{1}{L} & 0 & 0 & 0 & & \\
0 &             0 &            0 & 0 & \frac{1}{L} & \frac{1}{L} & & \\ \hline
0 &             0 &            0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & & \\
0 & \tfrac{1}{L}x-1 &  \tfrac{1}{L}x & 0 & 0 & 0 & & \\
0 &             0 &            0 & 0 & \tfrac{1}{L}x-1 & \tfrac{1}{L}x & & \\ \hline
0 &             0 &            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \frac{x}{L}-1 & \frac{x}{L} \\
0 &             0 &            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{L}   & \frac{1}{L} \\
\end{array}\right]
\begin{pmatrix}
N_j \\ 
M_{zi} \\ 
M_{zj}
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}
In the absence of element loads, the axial force is constant, while the bending moment varies linearly along the element.

From the principle of virtual forces, the compatibility relationship between section deformations and element deformations is

$$
\dd \mathbf{v}=\int_0^L \mathbf{b}^{\top} \mathbf{e} \, \dd x
$$

Linearization of Eq. (16) with respect to basic forces gives the element flexibility matrix

$$
\mathbf{F} \triangleq \frac{\partial \mathbf{v}}{\partial \mathbf{q}}=\int_0^L \mathbf{b}^{\top} \mathbf{f}_s \mathbf{b} \, \dd x
$$
where $\mathbf{f}_s=\mathbf{k}_s^{-1}$ is the section flexibility matrix. 

$$
0 =\delta \Pi=\left(\begin{array}{l}
\delta \tilde{\mathbf{a}} \\
\delta \tilde{\mathbf{q}} \\
\delta \tilde{\mathbf{e}}^l
\end{array}\right) \cdot \left(
\\
\begin{pmatrix}
   \mathbf{H}^{\mathrm{t}}\tilde{\mathbf{q}} \\
\mathbf{b}_l^{\mathrm{t}} \tilde{\mathbf{e}}^l - \mathbf{H} \tilde{\mathbf{a}} \\
  -\mathbf{b}_l \tilde{\mathbf{q}}
\end{pmatrix}
-\left(\begin{array}{c}
\mathbf{F} \\
\mathbf{0} \\
\mathbf{s}_l^p-\hat{\mathbf{s}}_l
\end{array}\right)
\right)
$$

$$

  +\delta \mathbf{q} \cdot \left(\mathbf{A} \mathbf{u}-\int_0^L \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{t}}  \bm{e} \mathrm{~d} x\right)
  +\delta \mathbf{u} \cdot \left(\left(\mathbf{A}^{\mathrm{t}}  \mathbf{q}-\overline{\mathbf{p}}_{\mathbf{w}}\right)-\mathbf{p}\right) \\

  +\delta \mathbf{u} \cdot \left(\left(\mathbf{A}^{\mathrm{t}}  \mathbf{q}-\bar{\mathbf{p}}_{\mathbf{w}}\right)-\mathbf{p}\right) \\
\end{aligned}
